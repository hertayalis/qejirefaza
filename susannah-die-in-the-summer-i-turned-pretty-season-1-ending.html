The Jenny Han Cinematic Universe continues with "The Summer I Turned Pretty," which will be released on Amazon Prime on June 17th.

Consider "The Summer I Turned Pretty" to be the TV version of a beach book, as opposed to "To All the Boys I Loved Before," which takes place during the school year.

The story of the series revolves around Isabel "Belly" Conklin, a young woman who becomes embroiled in a love triangle with two brothers, Conrad and Jeremiah. 

You'll be relieved to learn that the program has already been renewed for Season 2 if you binge-watched all seven episodes.

Han's publications provide insight into the series's future. Here's everything we learn about "The Summer I Turned Pretty" (series 2) thus far.

Spoilers: Does Susannah Die In The Summer I Turned Pretty? 

Conrad's mother Susannah dies of breast cancer in Season One, which she had kept hidden from her children until the dramatic end when her illness is revealed.

In the second story, set a year later, the atmosphere at the beach home is vastly altered. Belly's connection with the Fisher boys alters as a result of these issues.

They're still attempting to have a "normal" summer together, but it's becoming increasingly difficult.

Is The Summer I Turned Pretty Returning For Season 2?

The Summer I Turned Pretty, a new Prime Video show, was granted the light symbol for a second series even before it launched on Friday, June 17.

That could indicate that viewers haven't yet had the opportunity to watch the series and become fascinated with it, but it seems like they will.

According to Deadline, a second series will undoubtedly be developed. It hasn't been given a release date, but given that it was revealed in June this year, it's safe to assume it'll return in the summer of 2023.

[image-2]

Rachel Blanchard, Christopher Briney, Lola Tung, Jackie Chung, Gavin Casalegno, Sean Kaufman, Alfredo Narciso, Minnie Mills, Colin Ferguson, and Tom Everett Scott are among the newcomers to the show. 

Across Han's three-book series, Briney and Casalegno will return as the brothers who remain to vie for Belly's love.

Han will reappear as a showrunner behind the cameras, this time collaborating with Sarah Kucserka.

She has also written the pilot for the program and continues to have a big say in how the onscreen  portrayal of her story unfolds.
