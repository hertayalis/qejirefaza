Also, what happens if you use salted butter rather than unsalted butter?

Using salted butter instead of unsalted butter in a recipe will result in a baked dish with extra salt, if all other things are similar. If you want or need to use salted butter instead of unsalted butter in a dish that calls for salt, leave it out or use less salt than the recipe calls for.

So, instead of unsalted butter, may you use margarine?

1 cup margarine, 1 cup shortening plus 1/2 teaspoon salt, 7/8 cup vegetable oil plus 1/2 teaspoon salt, or 7/8 cup lard plus 1/2 teaspoon salt can be used for 1 cup salted butter.
